NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) are unique digital assets that are stored on a blockchain and represent ownership of a specific item or piece of content. NFTs are often used to represent digital art, collectibles, or other items that are unique and cannot be exchanged for an equal value. Here are a few things to know about NFTs: NFTs are stored on a blockchain: This means that the ownership of an NFT is verified and recorded on a decentralized, transparent ledger. NFTs are unique: Unlike cryptocurrencies, NFTs are unique and cannot be replicated or exchanged for an equal value. This makes NFTs ideal for representing unique digital assets like artwork or collectibles. NFTs can be bought and sold: NFTs can be bought and sold on various marketplaces, just like physical artwork or collectibles. The ownership of an NFT can be transferred from one person to another. NFTs are not just for art: While NFTs are often associated with digital art, they can also be used to represent a wide range of unique digital assets, including videos, music, and even virtual real estate.
